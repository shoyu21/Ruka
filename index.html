Sepsis / suspected infection
Gateway question: if there is no infection, the adsorptive (Oxiris) filter is not considered at all.
Oxiris is suggested only when both below are present — refractory septic shock with a high endotoxin / cytokine burden:
Gram-negative source or markedly raised inflammatory markers (abdominal / urinary endotoxin; or CRP >100 / PCT >2 / lactate >4)
Refractory vasopressors (NorA >0.5, ≥2 agents, or escalating)
Gram-negative (abdominal / urinary) is the stronger signal — Oxiris adsorbs endotoxin. Either box satisfies this gate.
Cardiac cause / known poor LV function (low EF, cardiogenic picture)
Filter unchanged — this only makes ultrafiltration more cautious (avoid preload depletion).

▸When would a senior move the dose — and why?DRAFT · pending sign-off

A higher dose does not improve survival — RENAL (40 vs 25), ATN (35 vs 20) and IVOIRE (70 vs 35 in sepsis) all show a flat dose–outcome curve above ~25 ml/kg/h delivered. So you move the dose only to solve a specific clearance problem, never because the patient “seems sicker.”

Move it UP — to clear a specific solute faster
  • Tumour lysis — K⁺, phosphate and urate produced faster than a standard dose clears.
  • Refractory hyperkalaemia — standard clearance isn’t holding K⁺ down.
  • Hyperammonaemia (liver failure, urea-cycle) — small, dialysable; clear hard to protect the brain.
  • Dialysable toxin / severe HAGMA — metformin, toxic alcohols, salicylate, lithium.
  • Rhabdomyolysis (myoglobin) — evidence thin; a clinician judgement, not a rule.
Cost of going up: also clears more phosphate / K⁺ / Mg (replace them) and more antibiotic — get TDM; underdosing kills.
Move it DOWN — for controlled correction or gentleness
  • Slow correction — dysnatraemia or severe uraemia, to avoid cerebral oedema / dialysis disequilibrium. Here the low dose is the therapy.
  • Haemodynamic instability — prioritise stability over clearance.
  • Stable / recovering — minimise over-dialysis (electrolyte & micronutrient loss).
Cost of going down: risk of subtherapeutic clearance — confirm you’re still controlling whatever needed controlling.
If none of the above applies: use REINA’s suggested value. It targets 20–25 ml/kg/h delivered — the flat, evidence-based part of the curve. Higher is neither safer nor better (RENAL / ATN / IVOIRE, HIGH grade).
